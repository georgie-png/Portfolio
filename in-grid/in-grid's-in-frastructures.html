In-Grid's In-frastructures

These inquiries offer up how critical access can be brought into collaborations within non-disabled centred communities, not as extra time, but as a way to disobediently wiggle room within infrastructuring practices for interdependent sense making and collective action. These inquiries take on the form of focus groups, accessible docs and a set of workshops called Practising Protocols that ran both In-ternally with In-grid as well as at 4S/EASST.

These inquiries aim to share how critical access and crip methods make room for a collective like In-grid to question our organisational and network infrastructures by both coming into contact with them, but also by shaping them from our backgrounds and expertise. In this inquiry especially bring focus to how disobedient critical access and disability can make room for the misfitting withing normalised relations that can be unfold into counter expertise and knowledges. With this approach making sense of the assimilationist norms and politics of configuration in a way that makes room for us to refuse, improvise and deviate from their norms.

In-fra Processing focus group

This entry shares a collective focus group with In-grid members to reflect on their organisational practices and approaches. In this session questioning how members made sense of and made friction around the social and institutional inflexibilities they misfit within and were impacted by when trying to organise together.

In-Docs

This entry reflects on a collective inquiry with In-grid members around how we practice and imagine our technical Docs and network infrastructures when informed by critical access. In these collective practices questioning how prescribed technical configurations and their isolated expertise can be disputed, improvised and refused when made accessible to other backgrounds, capacities and knowledges.

Collective Infrastructures Combined Panel

This entry shares a combined panel and workshop organised by In-grid members for 4S/EASST 2024. With this combined panel bringing together a panel of community practitioners to share their experiences of collective infrastructuring, alongside and accessible workshop which made room for participants to make-sense of and counter the configurations they encountered, misfit within and found friction with during the technical session.

Serving In-grid workshop

This entry shares a collective workshop where In-grid members came together to run the Practising Protocols workshop in-ternally. For this iteration we set up our first collective server as well as our first server manifesto called the Femfester. In this workshop making room to make-sense of our misfitting within our inherited configurations of network infrastructures as to manifest them otherwise.
