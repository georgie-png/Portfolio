Title: Is It Configure-Able?

Is It Configure-Able?

With my project I make room to inquire with collaborators if our infrastructures are Configure-Able. Informed by Queer theory, crip theory and critical access practices, this project works from the sites of friction within configurations to wiggle room into how these processes and relations can be shaped by the bodies they impact.

Orienting with Sarah Ahmed's table (2006), I make room to question how the institutional norms of computing and academia keep the life affirming infrastructures we desire out of reach. With Ahmed and Helen Pritchard's concept of Wiggle Room (2022; 2014) I question how to make room to feel these pains and misfitting of the table, and in response wiggle and disorient these tables towards disabled bodies, futures and politics. With Alison Kafer (2013) and Aimi Hamraie (2017), I surface the ways they begin to not only validate the feelings and sense making of friction within relations, but also the making of friction from embodied expertise and through interdependence.
