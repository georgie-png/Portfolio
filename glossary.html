Glossary

This Glossary aims to share some of the terms and words used to shape this research. These descriptions aim to accessibly offer up how this research works with crip and queer feminist theory to form critical access informed approaches to community infrastructuring. These terms help to understand how this research makes sense of the many bodies, technologies and relations that it is in touch with. This glossary brings focus to how this research has brought together, moved through and made friction with the unknowable and unpredictable actions of community infrastructuring.

Terms

Friction

Friction is a process of access making. It brings together both the making sense of when access is needed or when friction is felt, with the action of making access through friction, making request and transforming your feelings into change. In crip theory there is also this word play with friction, that even though there can be pain from frictions, there is also a possibility for pleasure and joy, which is often never imagined. Friction in this research is then understood as being a place of joyous, pleasurable and embodied access making in action.

Wiggle Room

Wiggle Room builds from Friction to pose a process of friction making. This process is specifically around the categories and relations that people are prescribed by the norms of systems. With Wiggle Room this process of friction making takes on the approach of feeling out how determined categories and relations misfit the bodies they impact. From this sense making asking how these impacted bodies can Wiggle Room within these determined relations and towards ones that are shaped by and affirm the lives they hold.

Table

The Table in this research is a metaphor for the institutional norms that have been gradually sedimented into determined and inflexible relations. Tables in this research are take many forms, from my desk, to excell tables, to kitchen tables, to interview tables, operating tables, and to the grid of cells on a video call. Working with the Table this research asks how these institutes and norms make room for us to be together, and how we can also start to push back and bring these norms in touch with out own tables and relations.

Cripped Table

The Crip Table builds from the Table to imagine what a Table shaped by crip politics and community would feel like. With this the Crip Table questions how tables can not be held in place, or oriented towards determined bodies and relations, but instead towards promises of life affirmation and justice. The Crip Table making room to imagine how systems, infrastructures and their of inflexible relations can be flexible and shaped by the bodies they impact and are in touch with.

Bodily Horizon

The Bodily Horizon in this research makes present and situates how bodies are in relation with things around them. The Bodily Horizon makes room to question what is within reach, what can be changed, and what limits and capacities there are within the actions we take. It both makes sense of the frictions of the present while making room to question what the body wants to be within reach of in the future. With the Bodily Horizon here, it is then a body that is in motion, one that feels and makes change, and one that sits between the present and the future.

Bodymind

The Bodymind in crip theory, is the refusal of the split between the mind and body made within western medicine and philosophy. It instead brings together the body and the mind in a way that brings to the foreground the body and it sense making, whilst still caring for the mind. In the breaking down of boundaries between mind and body is also extended in this research to break down the boundaries between bodies and the relations that impact them. In this orientation the Bodymind questions the determined ways bodies, minds and their matters are separated, and makes room to feel how they might come together and transform relations through their entangled sense making.
